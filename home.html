What you're looking at

This tab shows a machine learning model trained on 3 seasons of NC A&T box scores (2023-24, 2024-25, 2025-26) plus opponent ratings from ~18,800 D1 games via Bart Torvik's strength-of-schedule-adjusted efficiency data.

The model predicts how productive each player will be per 40 minutes of play rather than raw totals — this removes the bias where starters automatically look better just because they play more.

Training vs Testing: the model was trained on the first 80% of games chronologically (up to Jan 2026), then tested on the remaining 20% it had never seen. All accuracy numbers come from those held-out test games only — not games the model learned from.

How to read the numbers
  • MAE — average points/40 the model was off per game. Lower = more accurate.
  • R² — % of game-to-game variance explained. 0.232 means ~23% explained. Positive = real signal found.
  • vs Baseline — improvement over simply predicting each player's season average. +0.18 means the model saves 0.18 pts/40 of error per game vs doing nothing.
  • Predicted vs Actual scatter — dots on the diagonal = perfect prediction. Below diagonal = model under-predicted (player outperformed expectations).
Color guide (scatter chart)
  • ● Green — MAE < 3 pts/40 — model predicts this player accurately
  • ● Gold — MAE 3–6 pts/40 — reasonable prediction, within variance
  • ● Red — MAE > 6 pts/40 — model struggles (usually high-variance or trending players)

Minutes-Adjusted Rankings

Each chart compares a player's raw rank (total production) vs their per-40 rank (production per 40 minutes played). Gold bars = ranks higher when adjusted for minutes (under-utilised). Red bars = ranks lower (over-relied on). A bar of zero means minutes and productivity are aligned.

Scoring Rank — pts per game vs pts per 40

Corrects for the fact that starters score more simply by playing more minutes. Positive = player scores more efficiently than their raw total suggests.

Rebounding Rank — reb per game vs reb per 40

Rebounds are among the most stable and predictable stats — the model beats the baseline by 0.76 reb/40. Players with large positive bars are rebounding at an elite rate relative to their minutes.

Playmaking Rank — ast per game vs ast per 40

Assists per 40 reveals which players are genuine facilitators vs those who accumulate assists simply by being on the court more. R² 0.229 — the model explains real playmaking patterns.

Shooting Efficiency Rank — True Shooting %

True Shooting % accounts for 2s, 3s, and free throws in one number. No minutes adjustment needed — TS% is already a rate stat. This ranks who actually converts their shot attempts most efficiently.

Overall Offensive Impact Rank — composite pts + reb + ast per 40

Combines scoring (50%), rebounding (35%), and playmaking (15%) per 40 minutes into a single composite score — weighted by how strongly each stat beats the prediction baseline. This is the most complete picture of offensive contribution adjusted for minutes. Compare this to the raw totals ranking to find the most under- and over-utilised players overall.
